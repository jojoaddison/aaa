The AAA e.V. set itself the goal of afforestation and reforestation of forests in regions that currently have little or no trees due to extreme climatic conditions.

The primary goal is to show African communities how to sustainably harness natural resources, protect the environment whilst creating improved living conditions at the same time. Forests should have a wide range of trees. It is important to ensure that different types of trees such as palms, mahogany, other wood species as well as cashew and mango are combined to reduce the susceptibility to pests.

It might also be possible to plant trees from other continents that are able to survive even in these difficult climatic conditions. A successful cultivation of fruit and wood could improve local communities and the living standards of its people. In a global context afforestation projects should help to reduce global warming because trees can absorb carbon dioxide during photosynthesis and counteract the greenhouse effect.

Our Aims

The project is based on the needs of the local communities.

The project provides benefits that on sustainable and not temporal.

The project does not require the community to spend any of their own money but to contribute part of their land and do communal labor.

The project structure would address the needs of local families as well as bringing important benefit to larger environmental communities, e. g. reducing the threat of flooding, bushfires as well as stopping the rampant desert in Africa.

The project would therefore prevent irreparable destruction of forestry in Africa.

Our Objectives

The project centre would organize technical training for the youth.

The project will create jobs for the local people.

The project will develop yearly until experts have found the best trees for afforestation.

The centre would host national and international conferences.

Our Competence Center

To establish a competence center named:

"African Sustainable Afforestation Center"

The aim of the centre is to promote a strategic competence in community reafforestation and environmentally healthy establishment of forests in parts of Africa, where hitherto there were no trees, or reestablish forest in areas where it has disappeared. In doing so we want to apply modern methods of tree planting - using the waterboxx as a brilliant example alongside other traditional methods.

The centre is to be established in the town of Mafi-Anfoe found in the Volta Region of Ghana.
